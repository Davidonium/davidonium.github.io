Introduction

Jan 23 2021

About me#

Don’t much to say here. I am a software engineer based in barcelona who loves reading all kinds of text: fantasy novels, tech articles, tech books, reddit posts on /r/programming, everything on hackernews…

My love for computers started early in my life when I tinkered with a green phosphor screen computer, which brand I no longer remember. As I grew up there was always a computer at home to learn new things, the internet and all that. I was the typical millennial kid that loved playing games.

Contrary to a lot of fellow software engineers, I wasn’t good at programming until I learned it at the computer science degree, and didn’t improve much until the latest years. After the last year of university I spent the whole summer learning about technologies, building REST APIs, SOLID principles, dependency injection, etc. Things that I didn’t value much during my studies, but I found incredibly stimulating in an internship and afterwards at work. I learned more in that summer than I did in all my university years.

I’ve been working in software development right after my degree and I discovered that I love learning new things. The pure excitement from learning some new concept or a new programming language that felt right is like a drug to me. Software development allowed me to increase the ratio in which I discovered new things to learn, and I am still hooked to it.

Nowadays my main interests are backend development using either kotlin in the jvm ecosystem, go or php (mainly symfony). Most of the content I manage to write will be around those languages/environments.

Purpose#

I have been a hardcore lurker all my life and still am, absorbing knowledge from where I can, reading relentlessly without giving back. After some years of accumulating knowledge from work experience and a whole lot of reading, I think it is time to return the favor to the internets and share it.

After some years working in the software industry I’ve found out that I enjoy teaching new hires the ropes and share my learnings with them, the look on a coworker’s face when he figured out how a dependency injection container worked! Pure bliss. I hope that the blog posts I write will reach more people and help them love software development the same way I do.

Another reason to start writing is to get better at writing by itself and, since I am spanish and not a native english speaker, to get better at writing english. So bear with me if I mistype some words or explain myself too bluntly.

Final words#

So there’s the story of the start of my tech blogging life. No one cares, but I do it because I feel like it.

I hope some people find what I write here useful and comment, criticize and express your own opinions. I don’t want to maintain a comment platform or complicate the website, so feel free to reach me at twitter (link at the bottom) @davidonium.
